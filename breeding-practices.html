Ethical Mini American Shepherd Breeding Practices

We build every pairing around health, temperament, and breed preservation. Transparent testing, proven lines, and lifelong support.

Our Commitment

We prioritize the health, temperament, and well-being of our Mini American Shepherds above all else. Every breeding decision is made with careful consideration of genetic health, conformation standards, and breed preservation.

Health Testing Requirements

All breeding dogs undergo rigorous health screening before reproduction:

  • Orthopedic Evaluation: OFA and PennHIP screening for hip and elbow dysplasia
  • Eye Testing: CERF (Canine Eye Registration Foundation) certification
  • Genetic Screening: Testing for hereditary conditions including collie eye anomaly (CEA) and progressive retinal atrophy (PRA)
  • Cardiac Assessment: Screening for heart abnormalities
  • Genetic DNA Testing: Breed-specific genetic markers to ensure optimal genetic diversity

Breeding Standards

Our breeding program adheres to the American Kennel Club (AKC) and breed club guidelines:

  • Breed Standard Conformation: All breeding dogs meet or exceed breed standard specifications
  • Age Requirements: Dogs must be at least 2-3 years old before breeding
  • Limited Breeding Cycles: Females are bred no more than once per year and retire by age 8
  • Genetic Diversity: We maintain careful pedigree records to avoid inbreeding
  • Temperament Screening: All breeding dogs have stable, people-oriented temperaments

Why Health Testing Matters

Health testing in Mini American Shepherds prevents inheritable conditions and ensures puppies have the best possible start in life. Genetic disorders can be expensive to treat and cause suffering for both dogs and owners.

By choosing a responsible breeder who health tests, you invest in:

  • A longer, healthier life for your dog
  • Reduced veterinary bills associated with genetic conditions
  • Peace of mind knowing your puppy came from carefully selected genetics
  • Support for responsible breeding practices

Socialization & Early Development

Our puppies are raised in our home with early exposure to:

  • Multiple household environments
  • Various sounds and stimuli
  • Gentle human handling from birth
  • Age-appropriate play and learning
  • Initial training foundation

Lifetime Support & Responsibility

Our commitment doesn't end when puppies leave home. We provide:

  • Lifetime breeder support and consultation
  • Take-back policy if circumstances change
  • Ongoing advice on care, training, and health
  • Community with other Buddy relatives
